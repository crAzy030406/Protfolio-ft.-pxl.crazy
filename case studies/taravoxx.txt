PROJECT OVERVIEW
Overview

Tara Voxx is a digitally native astrology consultancy brand offering services related to:

astrology consultation,
vastu,
numerology,
and spiritual guidance.

The brand was in its early growth stage and aimed to establish itself as a trustworthy and premium alternative to rapidly growing astrology platforms in India.

The objective was to create a visual identity system that could:

build trust,
create strong recall,
feel premium and mature,
and emotionally connect with its niche audience.
Target Audience

Primarily users between the ages of 16–55 who strongly resonate with astrology, spirituality, vastu, and traditional belief systems.

THE CHALLENGE
The Challenge

Since the brand was in its initial phase, it lacked a recognizable visual identity that could represent its positioning in the astrology and spirituality market.

The client requested a logo system inspired by:

planets,
stars,
orbital motion,
and celestial symbolism.

However, the primary challenge was balancing:

luxury aesthetics,
spiritual symbolism,
trust perception,
and visual simplicity.

As a designer with a minimal design approach, the challenge was to avoid creating an identity that felt overly decorative or visually crowded while still preserving the emotional expectations of the target audience.

STRATEGIC DIRECTION
Strategic Direction

The identity needed to feel emotionally familiar to a mature audience while maintaining the sophistication of a modern premium brand.

Because the target audience primarily consisted of users aged 16–55, overly vibrant colors and aggressive typography were intentionally avoided.

The strategy focused on:

calm visual balance,
strong memorability,
premium contrast,
and emotionally resonant typography.

The visual language was designed to combine:

traditional emotional familiarity with modern premium aesthetics.

Very important sentence.

DESIGN EXPLORATION
Initial Exploration

The first logo direction was built using:

minimal celestial references,
elegant typography,
and restrained visual composition.

Although the identity appeared visually refined, it lacked strong memorability and failed to create a lasting emotional impact for the client and internal team.

This phase revealed an important insight:

minimal aesthetics alone were not enough for strong brand recall in this category.

That’s a VERY strong professional observation.

FINAL VISUAL DIRECTION
Final Identity Direction

The identity was redesigned with a bolder and more recognizable visual structure.

A star-inspired visual symbol was developed to create:

instant recall,
stronger visibility,
and a premium visual presence.

The combination of:

dreamy purple,
luxury gold,
and sharp celestial geometry

helped establish a visual identity that felt:

premium,
spiritual,
memorable,
and trustworthy.

As the brand evolved, additional graphic elements and supporting visuals were gradually introduced into the system to expand the identity consistently across platforms.

This is now sounding like a real branding project.

TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM
Typography System

Typography played a major role in establishing emotional trust and cultural familiarity.

Hindi Typography

The “Tara” wordmark was designed using the AMS Handwriting typeface in Hindi styling to create:

emotional connection,
cultural familiarity,
and a sense of spiritual authenticity.

This helped the audience relate to the brand more intuitively.

English Typography

The supporting typography used:

Futura for the primary English typography,
and Helvetica for secondary informational text.

Futura’s tall and structured appearance helped reinforce:

luxury,
sophistication,
and modernity.

Helvetica was used with thinner weights to maintain:

calmness,
readability,
and informational clarity.

Now it sounds intentional instead of “I used this font because premium.”

The supporting palette was developed as tonal variations of the primary colors to maintain consistency while improving visual flexibility across applications.

The final identity significantly improved the brand’s visual recognition and trust perception.

KEY LESSONS
Key Learnings

This project demonstrated that effective branding in spiritual and belief-driven industries requires more than aesthetic minimalism.

Key takeaways:

Emotional familiarity improves trust.
Memorability is essential in crowded digital markets.
Premium branding can coexist with traditional symbolism.
Strong visual systems evolve with the brand over time.
